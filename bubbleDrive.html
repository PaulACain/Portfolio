Bubble Drive

Bubble Drive is ground up build out of a cloud storage solution for small businesses for both web and mobile devices.

  • Role: User experience, branding, visual design
  • Tools & Software: Figma, InVision, Adobe creative suite
  • Deliverables: High fidelity mockups, branding guidlines, branding matieral

Overview

I was approached by a company seeking to move into the cloud storage market. My role was to identity an opportunity using research, then using design techniques, bring the product to life. I conducted market research, user surveys, and a variety of user testing throughout the process. In addition to that, I designed the products brand, logo, and webpage using UX design tools including Figma, inVision, and Adobe Illustrator.

The Problem

The client wanted me to explore a potential niche for a cloud storage solution that could leverage their business’s core strengths. Their project brief outlined some key features and attributes they liked from their experience with similar cloud storage application.

The solution

Using extensive research in the field and user tested theories, I was able to locate a market niche within the cloud storage space and develop a cloud storage application with features and processes to enables small businesses to thrive.

DISCOVERY & RESEARCH

Understanding the lay of the land

Competitive analysis

In order to understand where an opportunity might lie in the cloud storage space, I examined the major players in the landscape. What I became acutely aware of is how the market demands niche storage locations catering to a unique types of user. These specialized digital storage spaces are curated to specific people, industries, and even products.

With that in mind, I suggested to the client to leverage their position and create an application that caters to entrepreneurs and small businesses. You can find my whole competitive analysis report here.

User research

In order to learn more about potential users for this application, I created a user research survey. In this process I was attempting to learn following:

1. How familiar are people with cloud storage applications

2. What application features are most important to users

3. Is there is a market for a cloud storage tool in the small businesses space

After gathering 17 responses from individuals across the world from with a diverse background, I was able to determine the following from the surveys:

1. Users were extremely familiar with cloud storage applications with approximately nearly 50% using a product for work

2. The most important features for applications are ability to upload content, the ability to share and the ability to create content all within the application.

3. Of my 17 participants, 76% have worked in some capacity in a small business. Of those, 84% said that a cloud storage tool that allows owners/managers to share and collaborate with staff would be beneficial

User Persona

In an effort to translate the data from the survey into a useable representation of my ideal users, I created 2 primary personas. These helped me clarify the goal of my cloud storage solution: to create a platform that allows users to save, organize, and share content across business levels.

Branding & Identity

It all starts with a pencil

Logo design

My approach to designing a brand around a cloud storage application for small businesses was to follow the success of similar companies in the industry. Drawing inspiration from companies such as Homebase, Toast, and Wave, I developed the name Bubble Drive.

Key insight: I landed on a piece of gum for my logo design. My inspiration was derived from the Hubba Bubba bubble gum popular in the late 90’s. I was hoping to achieve a nostalgic yet fresh feel with the design. However, my clients didn’t feel the same way - which lead me back to the drawing board.

Logo design

Round 2

I realized I didn’t have a full understanding of the client’s brand expectation. I presented them with the logo using a piece of bubble gum as my inspiration. They did not like it. Though they were very happy with the idea and the name, they wanted more from the logo. The feedback they gave me listed out a couple of requirements for my next submission.

1. The logo needs to incorporate a bubble in some way

2. The logo needs to work as a stand alone icon

3. The logo should feel more professional

Key insight: Using the feedbcak derived from my clients, I created a series of bubble inspired logo icons and presented them to the client.
