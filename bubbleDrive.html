Bubble Drive

Bubble Drive is ground up build out of a cloud storage solution for small businesses for both web and mobile devices.

Role:
User experience, branding, visual design

Deliverables:
High fidelity mockups, branding guidlines, branding matieral

Tools & Software:
Figma, InVision, Adobe creative suite

Overview

I was approached by a company seeking to move into the cloud storage market. My role was to identity an opportunity using research, then using design techniques, bring the product to life. I conducted market research, user surveys, and a variety of user testing throughout the process. In addition to that, I designed the products brand, logo, and webpage using UX design tools including Figma, inVision, and Adobe Illustrator.

The Problem

The client wanted me to explore a potential niche for a cloud storage solution that could leverage their business’s core strengths. Their project brief outlined some key features and attributes they liked from their experience with similar cloud storage application.

The solution

Using extensive research in the field and user tested theories, I was able to locate a market niche within the cloud storage space and develop a cloud storage application with features and processes to enables small businesses to thrive.

DISCOVERY & RESEARCH

Understanding the lay of the land

Competitive analysis

In order to understand where an opportunity might lie in the cloud storage space, I examined the major players in the landscape. What I became acutely aware of is how the market demands niche storage locations catering to a unique types of user. These specialized digital storage spaces are curated to specific people, industries, and even products.

With that in mind, I suggested to the client to leverage their position and create an application that caters to entrepreneurs and small businesses. You can find my whole competitive analysis report here.

Key insight: The platform is positioned as the most compatible storage space available. It’s built into all Apple devices, works automatically, and is available at all times.

Key insight: The platform positions themselves as the worker friendly storage space with a focus on creative work. It’s considered a modern workspace designed to reduce busywork so users can focus on what matters.

Key insight: The platform positions themselves as the everyday storage solution. Utilizing their extensive digital services such as google docs, google slides, etc. they are able to offers the ability to quickly and easily save files to their cloud, for free.

User research

In order to learn more about potential users for this application, I created a user research survey. In this process I was attempting to learn following:

1. How familiar are people with cloud storage applications
2. What application features are most important to users
3. Is there is a market for a cloud storage tool in the small businesses space

After gathering 17 responses from individuals across the world from with a diverse background, I was able to determine the following from the surveys:

1. Users were extremely familiar with cloud storage applications with approximately nearly 50% using a product for work
2. The most important features for applications are ability to upload content, the ability to share and the ability to create content all within the application.
3. Of my 17 participants, 76% have worked in some capacity in a small business. Of those, 84% said that a cloud storage tool that allows owners/managers to share and collaborate with staff would be beneficial

User Persona

In an effort to translate the data from the survey into a useable representation of my ideal users, I created 2 primary personas. These helped me clarify the goal of my cloud storage solution: to create a platform that allows users to save, organize, and share content across business levels.

Branding & Identity

It all starts with a pencil

Logo design

My approach to designing a brand around a cloud storage application for small businesses was to follow the success of similar companies in the industry. Drawing inspiration from companies such as Homebase, Toast, and Wave, I developed the name Bubble Drive.

Key insight: I landed on a piece of gum for my logo design. My inspiration was derived from the Hubba Bubba bubble gum popular in the late 90’s. I was hoping to achieve a nostalgic yet fresh feel with the design. However, my clients didn’t feel the same way - which lead me back to the drawing board.

Logo design

Round 2

I realized I didn’t have a full understanding of the client’s brand expectation. I presented them with the logo using a piece of bubble gum as my inspiration. They did not like it. Though they were very happy with the idea and the name, they wanted more from the logo. The feedback they gave me listed out a couple of requirements for my next submission.

1. The logo needs to incorporate a bubble in some way
2. The logo needs to work as a stand alone icon
3. The logo should feel more professional

Key insight: Using the feedbcak derived from my clients, I created a series of bubble inspired logo icons and presented them to the client.

Building a brand

The guidlines

BC Alphapipe was chosen as our font text for its unique curvatures and dynamic apertures that continue with the brand identity of being fresh and engaging. Paired with Montserrat for its geometric, easy readability and variety of weights to help establish a hierarchy of information for the application.

Building a brand

The story

Bubble Drive is fresh take on traditional cloud storage logos. Playing off the notion of “fresh ideas daily”, the bubble also acts stands alone as an icon with a “b” shaped bubble. The logo is intended to represent the platform’s purpose, to be simple, easy, and most of all helpful.

Information architecture

Clarifying the content

Low fidelity wireframes

I wanted to test the low fidelity mockup for the most common user tasks derived from my user stories for an end goal of a more comprehensive MVP. Testing wireframes also provided me with useful site navigation data as I could watch users attempt to solve the tasks asked of them. Using InVison app to create a clickable prototype, I followed a written script to conduct four surveys. Test feedback informed me of top actions the application required:

1. Resize pages to avoid call to actions that require scroll down navigation to locate.
2. More obvious call to action buttons relating to their purposes. For example the a return to dashboard button
3. More obvious organizational prompts for files and folders.

User testing

Using my wireframes as a blueprint, I developed a high fidelity mockup focusing on page balance ancd visual hierarchy. I wanted to test the design preference on different elements of the application using a preference test. With the overarching focus being clear call to actions, overall page response and design fluidity.

I conducted 3 preference testings using InVision app and was to identify what design features worked better for the platforms purpose.

After having a better understanding of the users expectations for a product, I was able to make final adjustments to the prototype before conducting another user test.

Using a new clickable prototype, I once again tested the high importance user tasks to ensure proper function of the application. Using the same formula from the first user testing I was able to observe, monitor and capture insights from my participants. The key changes I needed to improve were:

1. Multiple interactions points for users to engage with their folders and files.
2. Clean and concise dashboard with resizing sections, headers and buttons.
3. Customization options for dashboard categories

User testing

Users loved the fresh feel of Bubble Drive’s platform. After spending 3 weeks researching, testing, and designing this project, my client was very happy with the result.

With more time, I would have liked to explore additional pages to make the prototype more exceptional for users. If time allowed, I would have created additional team pages and worked towards in app messaging system.

My time with this project has taught me the importance of a thorough design process. I wanted to help my client create a product that would help small business owners navigate the pitfalls of running their own business. And though I believe that was accomplished, using a more detailed approach is vital to creating an excellent product.
